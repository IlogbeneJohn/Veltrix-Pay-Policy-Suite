AWARENESS & TRAINING POLICY  
Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: 7th July, 2026  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO) & Training Manager  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  
________________________________________
1. Purpose  
The purpose of this Awareness & Training Policy is to ensure that all Veltrix Pay personnel are adequately trained to understand and fulfill their security responsibilities, with particular emphasis on fraud awareness and the handling of financial and cardholder data. This policy aligns with the NIST SP 800-53 AT (Awareness and Training) control family.  
________________________________________
2. Scope  
This policy applies to:  
•	All employees, contractors, interns, and third parties  
•	All individuals with access to Veltrix Pay systems, data, or facilities  
•	All information systems supporting payment processing and business functions  
________________________________________
3. Policy Statements  
3.1 Security Awareness Training  
•	All personnel must complete security awareness training:  
o	At onboarding  
o	Annually  
o	When significant threats or system changes occur  
•	Training must cover:  
o	Cybersecurity fundamentals  
o	Proper handling of sensitive information, including cardholder and account data  
o	Phishing, social engineering, and common threats  
o	Reporting procedures for incidents  
o	Recognizing fraudulent transaction patterns and social engineering targeting customer support  
3.2 Role-Based Training    
•	Personnel with specialized responsibilities (e.g., ISSO, administrators, developers) must receive role-specific training.  
•	Training must align with job responsibilities and NIST requirements.  
•	Privileged users must complete enhanced security training annually.  
•	Customer support and fraud operations staff must complete additional training on social engineering and account takeover prevention.  
•	Engineers working on the payment switch must complete PCI DSS secure development training annually.  
3.3 Continuous Training & Reinforcement  
•	Ongoing awareness activities must be conducted, including:  
o	Emails and alerts  
o	Posters  
o	Internal cybersecurity campaigns  
o	Simulated phishing exercises  
3.4 Phishing Simulation Program  
•	Personnel may be subject to regular phishing simulations.  
•	Results must be tracked to identify users who require additional training.  
•	High-risk individuals must receive targeted follow-up training.  
3.5 Documentation & Tracking  
•	All training activities must be documented in the organization's training system.  
•	Completion records must be retained for audits.  
•	Supervisors must ensure their personnel complete required training.  
3.6 Training Content Requirements  
Training materials must be:  
•	Up to date with emerging threats  
•	Aligned with NIST guidance and organizational policies    
•	Accessible to all personnel, including remote workers  
3.7 Third-Party Personnel  
•	Vendors and contractors must complete security training equivalent to internal personnel.  
•	Proof of training may be requested for compliance verification.  
________________________________________
4. Roles & Responsibilities  
Information Security Officer (ISO)  
•	Ensures training aligns with security policies and NIST controls  
•	Oversees phishing simulations and awareness initiatives  
Training Manager / HR  
•	Manages training delivery and record tracking  
•	Provides onboarding training  
Supervisors  
•	Ensure employees complete required training on time  
•	Identify additional training needs for their teams  
Employees  
•	Must participate in required training  
•	Must apply training in daily job functions  
•	Must report suspicious activities or threats  
________________________________________
5. Compliance  
Failure to complete required training may result in:  
•	Access restrictions  
•	Mandatory retraining  
•	Disciplinary action  
Non-compliance may also result in audit findings, regulatory issues, or increased security risk.  
________________________________________
6. Review Cycle  
This policy must be reviewed annually or upon significant changes in threats, technologies, or regulations.
