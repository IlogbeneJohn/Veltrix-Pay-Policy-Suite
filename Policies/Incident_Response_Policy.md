INCIDENT RESPONSE POLICY  
Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: 7th July, 2026  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO)  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  
________________________________________
1. Purpose  
The purpose of this Incident Response Policy is to ensure Veltrix Pay can detect, respond to, contain, and recover from cybersecurity incidents — including payment fraud and data breach events — in a timely and effective manner in accordance with NIST SP 800-61 and NIST SP 800-53 (IR control family).  
________________________________________
2. Scope  
This policy applies to:  
•	All Veltrix Pay information systems  
•	All employees, contractors, and third-party users  
•	All incidents affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability of organizational or customer financial data  
________________________________________
3. Policy Statements  
3.1 Incident Response Capability  
•	The organization must maintain a formal Incident Response Program.  
•	An Incident Response Team (IRT) must be established and trained, including representation from Fraud & Risk.  
•	Incident response tools (forensics, monitoring, ticketing) must be maintained.  
3.2 Incident Classification  
Incidents must be classified based on severity:  
•	High Severity: Data breach, ransomware, unauthorized access, suspected compromise of the payment switch or ledger  
•	Medium Severity: Malware infections, service disruption, confirmed fraud ring activity  
•	Low Severity: Policy violations, phishing attempts, isolated chargeback disputes  
3.3 Reporting Incidents  
•	All employees must report suspected incidents immediately.  
•	Incidents must be reported to:  
o	The Security Operations Center (SOC)  
o	The Information Security Officer (ISO)  
Reporting channels include:  
•	Email  
•	Phone hotline  
•	Incident ticketing system  
3.4 Incident Handling Process  
All incidents must follow the NIST IR lifecycle:  
1.	Preparation  
2.	Detection & Analysis  
3.	Containment  
4.	Eradication  
5.	Recovery  
6.	Post-Incident Activity  
3.5 Evidence Handling  
•	Evidence must be preserved using approved forensic procedures.  
•	Chain-of-custody must be documented for all evidence.  
•	Only authorized personnel may handle evidence.  
3.6 Communications  
•	Incident details must be shared only with authorized personnel.  
•	Public disclosures must be approved by senior leadership.  
•	Legal, compliance, and HR must be notified as required.  
•	Suspected breaches involving cardholder data must trigger notification to the acquiring bank and card networks per PCI DSS requirements.  
3.7 Documentation  
•	All incidents must be documented in the incident tracking system.  
•	An incident report must be completed within 72 hours of incident closure.  
•	Lessons learned must be documented within 10 business days.  
•	Confirmed breaches affecting customer personal or financial data must be reported to NITDA within 72 hours per the Nigeria Data Protection Act.  
3.8 Testing & Training  
•	Annual incident response exercises are required.  
•	Quarterly phishing simulations must be conducted.  
•	The IRT must complete annual training, including a dedicated fraud incident tabletop exercise.  
________________________________________
4. Roles & Responsibilities  
Information Security Officer (ISO)  
•	Oversees the Incident Response Program.  
•	Ensures compliance with regulatory reporting requirements.  
Incident Response Team (IRT)  
•	Responds to and contains incidents.  
•	Performs forensics and recovery activities.  
Security Operations Center (SOC)  
•	Monitors for anomalous activity.  
•	Performs detection, triage, and escalation.  
Fraud & Risk Team  
•	Investigates suspected fraud-related incidents.  
•	Coordinates with the IRT on financial impact assessment.  
System Owners  
•	Support containment, mitigation, and recovery efforts.  
•	Ensure proper system documentation is available.  
Employees  
•	Report incidents immediately.  
•	Cooperate during investigations.  
________________________________________
5. Compliance  
Failure to follow this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination of access, and potential legal liability.  
________________________________________
6. Review Cycle
This policy must be reviewed annually or whenever significant changes occur in the threat landscape, systems, or organizational structure.
