RISK ASSESSMENT POLICY  
Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: 7th July, 2026  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO)  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  
________________________________________
1. Purpose  
The purpose of this Risk Assessment Policy is to ensure that risks to Veltrix Pay operations, assets, customers, and mission are identified, analyzed, monitored, and managed in accordance with NIST SP 800-30, NIST SP 800-37, and NIST SP 800-53 RA control family.  
________________________________________
2. Scope  
This policy applies to:  
•	All Veltrix Pay information systems  
•	All personnel involved in system design, implementation, and operation    
•	All contractors and third parties who interact with organizational systems  
•	All risk assessments performed throughout the system lifecycle  
________________________________________
3. Policy Statements  
3.1 Conducting Risk Assessments  
•	Risk assessments must be performed:  
o	During system initiation (RMF Step 1 & 2)  
o	When significant changes occur  
o	Annually, at minimum  
o	Following security incidents  
o	Prior to launching new payment products or integrations  
•	Assessments must follow the NIST risk assessment methodology.  
3.2 Risk Identification  
Risk identification must include:  
•	Threat sources (internal, external, environmental)  
•	Vulnerabilities (technical, procedural, physical)  
•	Likelihood of occurrence  
•	Potential impacts, including financial loss, regulatory penalty, and reputational damage  
3.3 Risk Analysis & Rating  
•	Risks must be assessed by evaluating the likelihood and impact of potential events.  
•	Risks must be categorized as:  
o	Low  
o	Moderate  
o	High  
•	Assessment results must be documented in the Risk Assessment Report (RAR).  
3.4 Risk Response  
Approved risk responses include:  
•	Mitigate (implement security controls)  
•	Accept (document justification & approval)  
•	Transfer (outsourcing, insurance, or processor liability shift)  
•	Avoid (eliminate the risk source)  
All responses must be approved by system owners and the ISO.  
3.5 Risk Monitoring  
•	Risks must be continuously monitored throughout the system lifecycle.  
•	New or emerging risks must be evaluated promptly, including emerging fraud typologies.  
•	Risk tracking must include assigned owners, deadlines, and progress updates.  
3.6 Use of Tools  
•	Automated security tools (scanning, SIEM, dashboards, fraud scoring engines) must support ongoing risk awareness.  
•	Tools must be regularly updated and validated.  
3.7 Reporting Requirements  
•	Risk findings must be reported to leadership quarterly.  
•	High-risk findings must be escalated immediately.  
•	Risk reports must align with the System Security Plan (SSP) and POA&M.  
________________________________________
4. Roles & Responsibilities  
Information Security Officer (ISO)  
•	Oversees risk assessment process  
•	Ensures alignment with NIST requirements  
•	Approves risk acceptance and remediation decisions  
System Owners  
•	Participate in risk assessments  
•	Approve remediation strategies    
Security Team  
•	Conducts risk assessments  
•	Provides risk scoring  
•	Tracks remediation  
Employees & Contractors  
•	Must report new or emerging risks  
•	Must cooperate with assessment activities  
________________________________________
5. Compliance  
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, system access revocation, contract penalties, or regulatory consequences.  
________________________________________
6. Review Cycle  
This policy must be reviewed annually or when major system, organizational, or regulatory changes occur.
